## Treatment Targets

### Hypertension Treatment Targets

**General Population**:
- Target BP: <140/90 mmHg
- Home BP: <135/85 mmHg

**High-Risk Groups** (diabetes, kidney disease):
- Target BP: <130/80 mmHg
- Home BP: <125/75 mmHg

**Elderly Patients** (>65 years):
- Target BP: <150/90 mmHg
- Can be lowered to <140/90 mmHg if well tolerated

### Diabetes Control Targets

**Blood Glucose Control**:
- HbA1c: <7.0% (general target)
- Fasting glucose: 4.4-7.0 mmol/L
- 2-hour postprandial glucose: <10.0 mmol/L

**Individualized Targets**:
- Young, newly diagnosed: HbA1c <6.5%
- Elderly, multiple comorbidities: HbA1c <8.0%
- History of severe hypoglycemia: More relaxed target

**Other Indicators**:
- Blood pressure: <140/90 mmHg
- LDL-C: <1.8 mmol/L (or <1.4 mmol/L for very high risk)
- BMI: 18.5-24.9 kg/m²

### COPD Management Targets

**Symptom Control**:
- CAT score: <10 points (mild impact)
- mMRC score: Grade 0-1

**Lung Function**:
- FEV1: Maintain as much as possible
- Reduce exacerbations: ≤1 moderate exacerbation per year

**Exercise Capacity**:
- 6-minute walk distance: Improve or maintain
- Daily activities: Unrestricted

## Medication Management

### Common Hypertension Medications

**Drug Classes**:
1. **ACEI/ARB**: Prils, -sartans
2. **CCB**: Amlodipine, Nifedipine
3. **Diuretics**: Hydrochlorothiazide, Indapamide
4. **Beta-blockers**: Metoprolol, Bisoprolol

**Medication Principles**:
- Start with low dose
- Long-acting preparations preferred
- Combination therapy
- Individualized treatment
- Regular evaluation and adjustment

### Common Diabetes Medications

**Oral Hypoglycemic Agents**:
1. **Metformin**: First-line choice
2. **Sulfonylureas**: Glimepiride, Gliclazide
3. **Glinides**: Repaglinide
4. **Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors**: Acarbose
5. **DPP-4 inhibitors**: Sitagliptin
6. **SGLT-2 inhibitors**: Dapagliflozin
7. **GLP-1 receptor agonists**: Liraglutide

**Insulin**:
- Basal insulin
- Bolus insulin
- Premixed insulin

### Common COPD Medications

**Bronchodilators**:
1. **LAMA**: Tiotropium
2. **LABA**: Salmeterol, Formoterol
3. **LABA+LAMA**: Combination preparations

**Inhaled Corticosteroids**:
- ICS+LABA combination preparations
- For frequent exacerbations

**Others**:
- Theophylline
- Expectorants
- Antibiotics (during exacerbations)

## Lifestyle Interventions

### Hypertension

**DASH Diet**:
- High fruits and vegetables
- Low-fat dairy products
- Whole grains
- Poultry, fish, beans
- Nuts and seeds
- Low sodium, high potassium

**Other Recommendations**:
- Limit sodium <2g/day
- Regular exercise (150 minutes moderate-intensity per week)
- Limit alcohol
- Quit smoking
- Weight management (BMI <24)

### Diabetes

**Dietary Management**:
- Balanced diet
- Control carbohydrates
- Low GI foods
- Regular timing and portions
- Limit simple sugars

**Exercise Recommendations**:
- 150 minutes moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week
- 2-3 resistance training sessions per week
- Avoid prolonged sitting
- Post-meal exercise

**Other**:
- Quit smoking
- Limit alcohol
- Weight management
- Foot care
- Regular screening

### COPD

**Breathing Training**:
- Pursed lip breathing
- Diaphragmatic breathing
- Respiratory muscle training

**Exercise Rehabilitation**:
- Aerobic exercise
- Upper limb training
- Lower limb training
- Breathing exercises

**Other**:
- Quit smoking (most important)
- Nutritional support
- Vaccinations
- Avoid triggers

## Complication Screening

### Hypertension Complications

**Target Organ Damage Assessment**:
- Heart: ECG, echocardiogram
- Blood vessels: Carotid ultrasound, PWV
- Kidneys: eGFR, urine albumin
- Fundus: Fundus examination
- Brain: Head CT/MRI

**Screening Frequency**:
- Annual target organ damage assessment
- Follow-up every 3-6 months

### Diabetes Complications

**Microvascular Complications**:
- Retinopathy: Annual fundus examination
- Nephropathy: Annual eGFR, urine albumin
- Neuropathy: Annual nerve screening

**Macrovascular Complications**:
- Cardiovascular: Risk assessment
- Cerebrovascular: Symptom assessment
- Peripheral vascular: Foot examination

**Screening Frequency**:
- Annual comprehensive complication screening

### COPD Complications

**Common Complications**:
- Cardiovascular disease
- Osteoporosis
- Muscle atrophy
- Depression and anxiety
- Lung cancer (smokers)

**Monitoring**:
- Regular lung function
- Cardiovascular assessment
- Bone density screening
- Mental health assessment
