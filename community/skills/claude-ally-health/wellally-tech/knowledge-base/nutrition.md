## Frequently Asked Questions

### Basic Nutrition Knowledge

**Q: What is a balanced diet?**
A: A balanced diet includes appropriate amounts of macronutrients and sufficient micronutrients. General recommendations:
- Carbohydrates: 45-65% of total calories
- Protein: 10-35% of total calories
- Fat: 20-35% of total calories

**Q: How much water should I drink daily?**
A: Generally recommended 1.5-2 liters daily. Specific needs vary by weight, activity level, and climate.

**Q: How to calculate basal metabolic rate?**
A: Use the Mifflin-St Jeor formula:
- Men: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) - 5×age + 5
- Women: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) - 5×age - 161

### Diet Tracking

**Q: How to accurately track diet?**
A:
1. Use WellAlly's food database
2. Record food and portions for each meal
3. Use a kitchen scale for weighing
4. Record cooking methods

**Q: How to track when dining out?**
A:
1. Estimate food portions
2. Choose similar food items
3. Record possible seasonings and oil amounts
4. Add appropriate estimation error margin

### Special Diets

**Q: What should diabetics eat?**
A: Follow these principles:
1. Control total carbohydrate intake
2. Choose low-GI foods
3. Distribute carbohydrates evenly
4. Eat at regular times and in fixed amounts

**Q: What should hypertensive patients pay attention to in their diet?**
A: DASH diet principles:
1. Low sodium (daily <2g sodium)
2. High potassium, magnesium, calcium
3. Eat more fruits and vegetables
4. Choose low-fat dairy products
